Epic vs Ikon: Let's end the confusion

Hey {{name}},

"Should we get the Epic Pass or Ikon Pass?"

I get asked this constantly. Here's the honest answer: It depends entirely on where you want to ski.

Forget the marketing hype. Here's the real breakdown:

EPIC PASS

Best if you want to ski...

  • Vail, Beaver Creek, Breckenridge, Keystone (Colorado)
  • Park City (Utah)
  • Whistler (Canada)
  • European Alps: Verbier, Les 3 Vallees, Zermatt
  • Japan: Hakuba, Rusutsu, Niseko

Family highlight: Kids ski free (under 5) at all resorts

IKON PASS

Best if you want to ski...

  • Aspen/Snowmass, Steamboat, Copper (Colorado)
  • Big Sky, Jackson Hole (Wyoming/Montana)
  • Deer Valley, Alta, Snowbird (Utah)
  • Mammoth, Palisades Tahoe (California)
  • European: Chamonix, Zermatt, St. Anton

Family highlight: Deer Valley (amazing ski school) + Big Sky (family-friendly terrain)

The 3-Question Decision Framework

1. Where do you want to ski most?

Pick the pass that includes your #1 resort.

2. How many days will you ski?

7+ days? Pass is worth it. Less? Buy day tickets.

3. Do you live near a partner resort?

Local access multiplies the value significantly.

Hot take: You might not need either

If you're only skiing one trip to Europe or Japan, skip the pass. European multi-day tickets are often a better deal, and you won't be locked into the "pass partner" resorts.
