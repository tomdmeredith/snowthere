Wait... the Alps can be CHEAPER than Colorado?

Hey {{name}},

It sounds backwards. "Fly across the ocean to SAVE money?" But stick with the numbers here.

Real costs for a family of 4 (2 adults, 2 kids) for a 5-day ski trip:

Vail, CO St. Anton, Austria
Flights $1,600 $2,800
Lodging (5 nights) $2,500 $1,400
Lift tickets (5 days) $2,400 $1,100
Food & drinks $1,000 $700
Rentals $600 $400
TOTAL $8,100 $6,400

Save $1,700 AND ski 300+ runs

St. Anton has 300 km of runs vs Vail's 234 km

Why does this happen?

  • European lift tickets are ~50% cheaper - A day at Vail without Epic Pass is $250+. St. Anton? About $75.
  • Lodging is more competitive - Austrian guesthouses and pensions are half the price of Vail condos.
  • On-mountain food is reasonable - $15 schnitzel vs $30 mediocre burger.
  • Kids often ski free - Under 6 ride free at most European resorts.

Real talk: Flying further IS harder with kids. Jet lag is real. If you have limited vacation days, Colorado might still make sense. But if you have the time? Europe is genuinely the better value.
