Your kids' ages change EVERYTHING

Hey {{name}},

I see parents make this mistake all the time: They pick a resort based on someone else's recommendation without considering that their kids are a completely different age.

A resort that's perfect for a 4-year-old learning to pizza might be a disaster for a 12-year-old who wants to hit the terrain park.

Here's my quick guide by age:

Ages 3-5: The Magic Carpet Years

What matters most: Quality ski school, proximity to lodging, non-ski activities

Look for: Dedicated learning areas, magic carpets, half-day lesson options (their attention spans are short!), childcare for non-ski days

Best picks: Keystone, Deer Valley, Les Gets, Serfaus

Ages 6-9: The Confidence Builders

What matters most: Blue run variety, progression terrain, on-mountain lunch spots

Look for: Long cruiser runs, adventure trails with fun features, family-friendly intermediate terrain

Best picks: Park City, Zermatt, La Rosi√®re, Lech

Ages 10-13: The Speed Demons

What matters most: Terrain parks, challenging runs, ability to explore independently

Look for: Progression parks, varied terrain, reliable snow (they'll want to ski ALL day)

Best picks: Mammoth, Whistler, Val Thorens, St. Anton

Ages 14+: The Independent Rippers

What matters most: Big terrain, challenging runs, good cell coverage (for meeting up)

Look for: Off-piste options, parks and pipes, night skiing, cool apres-ski

Best picks: Jackson Hole, Chamonix, Verbier, Niseko

Got kids in different age brackets?

Look for resorts with excellent ski school programs AND varied terrain. Zermatt, Park City, and Lech are all great for mixed-age families. The key is finding somewhere everyone can have a great day - even if you're on different parts of the mountain.
